Jarosław Grzędowicz

Jarosław Grzędowicz was born on May 3rd, 1965 in Wrocław, Poland. When he was 17 years old he debuted with short story “Azyl dla starych pilotów” published in “Odgłosy” magazine. Since then he wrote few other stories and participated in creating and promoting of polish fantastic literature. His writing carier flourished in 2003 when his short stories collection titled “Księga jesiennych demonów” was published. Two years later the first volume of his monumental and brilliant novel “Pan Lodowego Ogrodu” reached daylight and was honoured with many prestigious prizes.

Books

Jarosław Grzędowicz’s stories are pure pleasure to read not only for fantasy and science-fition fans. His books are written in beautiful language and are full of unique and interesting alternative worlds, creative interpretation of myths and culture, exciting plot twists and last but not least sense of humor.

Prizes

His most famous novel “Pan Lodowego Ogrody” has been honoured with many prizes.

  • Zajdel’s prize for best novel
  • Sfinks Literature Prize for best polish novel
  • Śląkfa Prize for the creator of the year
  • Fahrenheit Prize for best work
